Our Mission

The mission of Kings County Ultimate Disc Association (KCUDA) is to promote, develop, and support the sport of Ultimate Frisbee in Brooklyn and throughout New York City by:

  • Providing middle and high school aged students from Brooklyn and New York City with opportunities to participate in competitive Ultimate Frisbee at local, regional, and national levels.
  • Fostering an inclusive and supportive community that embraces the spirit of the game, emphasizing sportsmanship, fair play, and personal growth.
  • Educating and training players and coaches to enhance the quality and accessibility of Ultimate in our area.
  • Collaborating with schools, parks departments, and other organizations to increase awareness and participation in Ultimate Frisbee across diverse communities in Brooklyn and NYC.

Our Teams

Brooklyn Tech Support

Brooklyn Tech Support is the open high school ultimate team made up of students from Brooklyn Technical High School. The team has been active since 2019-2020, though Brooklyn Tech has fielded teams going back to the 1980s when NYC was one of the initial hubs of high school ultimate.

Teams

Tech Support fields both A team (varsity, try-out based) and B team (JV, open to any player) that compete throughout the year.

Season Structure

The team plays throughout the school year (September to May), with the primary competitive season in the spring, culminating in State championships over Memorial Day Weekend.

Practice Schedule

Practices are typically held on Tuesdays (4:30pm), Thursdays (4:30pm), and Saturdays (2pm) at Prospect Park Parade Grounds, with winter practices at lit fields at McCarren and Brooklyn Bridge Park.
